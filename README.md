# Healthcare Data Analytics Project

## Project Overview

This project analyzes real-world healthcare data published by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). Using Python, I cleaned, explored, and analyzed multiple datasets to identify trends in hospital quality, patient satisfaction, Medicare spending, and healthcare performance across the United States.

The project demonstrates an end-to-end data analytics workflow, including data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualization, correlation analysis, and cross-dataset integration.

---

## Business Questions

This project aims to answer several healthcare analytics questions, including:

- Which states have the highest and lowest hospital complication scores?
- Which hospitals perform best and worst across quality metrics?
- Does higher Medicare spending lead to better patient satisfaction?
- How does patient satisfaction vary across states?
- What trends exist in healthcare quality and outcomes over time?

---

## Datasets

The project uses publicly available CMS datasets:

- Hospital Complications and Deaths
- HCAHPS Patient Satisfaction
- Medicare Hospital Spending per Patient
- Timely and Effective Care

**Source:** Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)

## Project Workflow

1. Loaded and inspected four real-world healthcare datasets.
2. Cleaned and standardized missing values and data types.
3. Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA).
4. Created visualizations to compare hospitals and states.
5. Conducted correlation and regression analysis.
6. Combined multiple datasets to investigate relationships between healthcare spending and patient satisfaction.
7. Summarized findings and business insights.

---

## Key Findings

- Medicare spending showed a weak negative correlation with patient satisfaction.
- Hospital complication rates varied across states.
- Patient satisfaction differed significantly among hospitals and regions.
- Timely and effective care performance also varied geographically.
- Data cleaning was essential before meaningful analysis could be performed.
